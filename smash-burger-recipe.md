# 🍔 Smash Burger Recipe

## What's a Smash Burger?
A thin, crispy-edged burger made by smashing a ball of ground beef flat onto a hot griddle or cast iron pan. Maximum crust, juicy interior, simple ingredients.

---

## Ingredients (per burger)
- 80–100g **ground beef** (80/20 fat ratio — don't go lean!)
- 1 soft **burger bun** (potato rolls are king)
- 1–2 slices **American cheese**
- **Salt & pepper** (just before smashing)
- Optional: thinly sliced onions, pickles, mustard, ketchup

---

## Method

1. **Heat** a cast iron skillet or flat-top griddle until **ripping hot** — like, smoking hot.
2. **Form** the beef loosely into a ball. Don't overwork it.
3. **Place** the ball on the hot surface, then **smash it hard** with a sturdy spatula (use a second spatula or a wooden spoon to press down) until it's thin — about 1 cm or less.
4. **Season** immediately with salt and pepper.
5. **Cook 60–90 seconds** until edges are brown and crispy, then **scrape under it well** and flip.
6. **Place cheese** on top right after flipping.
7. **Cook another 30–60 seconds**, then stack and serve on a toasted bun.
8. If making a double: repeat and stack 'em.

---

## Tips
- **Don't smash ahead of time** — smash once, directly on the hot cooking surface.
- That brown crust (the *Maillard reaction*) is the whole point. If it's not crispy, your pan wasn't hot enough.
- Toast the bun in the beef fat left on the griddle.
- Keep it simple. Smash burgers don't need fancy toppings.
